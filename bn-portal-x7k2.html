AI Interviews

Warm, conversational pre-screening — candidates say it feels like talking to a person

Question Set — Senior Care Assistant
Sample Interview — Michael Chen
Score: 92%

Interviewer: Hi Michael, thanks so much for making time today — I know shifts can make scheduling tricky. Before we get into anything formal, how's your week been?

Michael: Good thanks! Just finished a run of nights, so enjoying a couple of days off.

Interviewer: Nights take it out of you, so well earned. So, I saw you've spent about five years in learning disability support. What first drew you into care work?

Michael: My younger brother has autism, so I grew up understanding what good support looks like — and what it feels like when it's missing.

Interviewer: That's a really personal way in, thank you for sharing that. Can you walk me through a shift that stuck with you — maybe one where things didn't go to plan, and how you handled it?

Michael: There was a resident who became very distressed during a routine change. I sat with him, matched his pace, and we got there in his own time rather than mine.

Interviewer: "In his own time rather than mine" — that's exactly the mindset our clients look for. Last thing before we talk practicalities: what would your current colleagues say it's like to work alongside you?

AI assessment: Empathetic, person-centred, calm under pressure. Notice-period two weeks. Recommended to progress. Interview conducted conversationally — no scripted or robotic phrasing detected by candidate-experience check.
