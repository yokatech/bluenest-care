Our Story

Built by a Carer.
Built for the Entire UK.

BlueNest Care Recruitment Ltd was founded in Sheffield by Eric Kalongo, a care sector professional who worked as a frontline carer and witnessed first-hand the chronic staffing crisis facing homes. Having seen how much hardworking carers deserve better support, Eric launched BlueNest with a clear, national ambition: to build the UK's most trusted care staffing agency, starting right here in our local community.

Where we started

A problem experienced from the inside

We did not build BlueNest Care from a boardroom. We built it from the care home floor, drawing on our own experience as a frontline carer. We worked the same last-minute calls, covered the same understaffed shifts, and experienced first-hand the pressures faced daily by exhausted care teams across the UK care sector.

What stood out to us was not a lack of committed carers, but rather a fragmented system that failed to connect them effectively with the care homes that needed them most. Capable and dedicated carers were often left waiting for shifts, while care home managers relied on costly national agencies that had limited understanding of their residents, values, or local culture.

BlueNest Care was founded in 2026 to address this disconnect, beginning in Sheffield, where the need was both urgent and deeply personal, and where building strong local relationships made the greatest impact.

Our national mission

From Sheffield to every corner of the United Kingdom

The UK care sector employs approximately 1.7 million people while facing an estimated 152,000 vacancies at any given time. With an annual staff turnover rate of around 28%, care providers nationwide face an ongoing workforce challenge stretching from Glasgow to Cardiff, London to Leeds, across both rural care homes and urban nursing facilities

BlueNest Care was built with a national vision to address this crisis head-on. While proudly rooted in Sheffield and South Yorkshire, we are building a nationwide healthcare staffing network designed to connect exceptional care professionals with the providers who need them most. Our mission is to deliver consistent staffing quality, absolute compliance transparency, and a human-centred partnership to care facilities in every postcode across the United Kingdom.

To drive this expansion, BlueNest Care is continuously scaling its core infrastructure, including our rapid compliance vetting systems, digital shift-matching capabilities, and regional talent pipelines. This robust foundation ensures that as we grow, healthcare providers across the UK can instantly access reliable, shift-ready staff to protect their operational continuity and alleviate rota pressures.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026 • BlueNest Care Recruitment Ltd

BlueNest Care Recruitment Ltd ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, process, and protect your personal data when you use our recruitment services, website, or apply for care roles within the UK.

1. Data We Collect About You

We may collect, use, store, and transfer different kinds of personal data about you, including:

  • Identity Data: First name, last name, title, date of birth, and proof of identification (passport, driving licence, or right to work document).
  • Contact Data: Billing address, email address, telephone numbers, and residential history.
  • Professional & Compliance Data: CV, work history, references, professional certifications (NVQ, Care Certificate), Enhanced DBS status, and training records.

2. How We Use Your Data

We use your information under the statutory guidelines of the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018:

  • To process and evaluate candidate applications for open healthcare vacancies.
  • To verify identity, qualifications, reference checks, and compliance clearances.
  • To link care homes requiring staffing support directly with active personnel profiles.

3. Data Retention and Safeguarding

All sensitive information (including passport scans, reference details, and background checks) is encrypted and saved securely within protected cloud infrastructures with multi-factor authentication. We retain applicant and client details only as long as necessary to fulfill corporate contractual needs or to comply with statutory financial and employment records retention laws.

5. Your Legal Rights

Under UK data protection laws, you hold clear rights regarding your personal information, including the right to request access to your records, request corrections to incorrect data, object to specific processing paths, or request data erasure under certain legal parameters. To exercise any of your data protection rights, please contact us directly at info@bluenestcare.co.uk.

3.1 The Agency warrants that all Staff deployed undergo stringent vetting prior to placement, including: verified Right to Work in the UK, an Enhanced DBS check (and/or registered Update Service tracking), comprehensive reference verification, and verified mandatory training compliance (including Safe Moving & Handling, Safeguarding, and Basic Life Support).

3.2 The Agency will provide a digital Compliance Profile containing necessary verification evidence to the Client prior to or at the commencement of a scheduled shift assignment.

4. CHARGES, PAYROLL, & INVOICING

4.1 The Client agrees to pay the hourly charge rates agreed upon at the time of booking, which are subject to variations based on shift timing (days, nights, weekends, or bank holidays).

4.2 Invoices are issued weekly by the Agency based on signed timesheets. The Client must pay all undisputed invoices within thirty (30) days from the date of the invoice.

4.3 In compliance with UK Conduct Regulations, the Agency remains legally liable for processing and distributing timely payroll to all temporary Staff regardless of any outstanding or delayed invoice payments from the Client.

5.1 If a Client cancels a confirmed shift booking with less than twenty-four (24) hours' notice, the Agency reserves the right to charge a standard cancellation fee equivalent to four (4) hours of the agreed shift rate.

5.2 The Agency enforces a mandatory 2-hour pre-shift check-in window for all Staff. In the rare event of a sudden staff emergency or absence, the Agency will make every effort to deploy an on-call backup worker immediately and notify the Client transparently.

6. TEMP-TO-PERM TRANSFER FEES

6.1 If the Client engages or employs a member of the Agency’s Staff directly or via another third party within six (6) months of that worker’s last assigned shift with the Client, the Client shall pay the Agency a standard Transfer Fee or agree to an extended period of hire, as specified in our fee schedule.
